Integrated Human Practices

Persistent organic pollutants (POPs) are an emerging problem in both human health and the ecosystem. Even though many of these compounds have been regulated under the Stockholm Convention since 2004 [ref1], today POPs are still found in circulation in drinking water and wastewater [ref2, ref3], among other water sources in Sweden. We aim to contribute to the management of POP levels in aquatic environments by developing a novel method for the detection and differentiation of POPs.

Throughout the development of S-POP, we have consulted with experts and stakeholders within academia, industry, legislation, healthcare and society all serving to create a product that would benefit society. The academic sector played a vital part in developing the technical aspects of our project. Input from the industry and legislative authorities helped in shaping the application of the project. Finally, discussions with the health sector and society gave us valuable input on factors to consider when implementing S-POP.
